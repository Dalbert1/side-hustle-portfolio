## Design Context

### Users
Small group of friends (up to 5) who play Old School RuneScape together. They do varied content - bossing, raids, minigames - and need a quick way to recall what gear/inventory setups to bring without spending time on the wiki. Used before logging into the game or while playing on a second monitor.

### Brand Personality
Rugged, Functional, Trustworthy

### Emotional Goal
Confident preparation - the feeling of knowing you're ready before you start an activity.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Dark, clean, data-focused. Inspired by WiseOldMan.net - utilitarian but polished, not flashy.
- **Theme**: Dark mode only. Medieval undertones but modern execution. Not a fantasy game skin - a tool that respects the game's aesthetic without cosplaying it.
- **References**: WiseOldMan.net (clean dark data UI, OSRS-aware but not over-themed)
- **Anti-references**: Overly flashy gaming sites with neon gradients, generic Bootstrap dashboards, anything that screams "AI made this"
- **Colors**: Gold (#c8aa6e) as primary accent on deep dark surfaces. Warm parchment tones for text. No cyan, no purple gradients, no neon.

### Design Principles
1. **Function over form** - Every element earns its place. No decorative filler.
2. **Quiet confidence** - The UI should feel solid and reliable, not showy. Think well-worn leather, not chrome.
3. **Scannable** - Users are looking up setups quickly, often mid-game. Information density over whitespace.
4. **Game-aware, not game-themed** - Use OSRS terminology and structure naturally without cartoonish game-skin aesthetics.
5. **Zero friction** - Minimize clicks to get to a setup. Search and browse should be instant.
